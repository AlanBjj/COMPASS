FALSE PREMISE: the main question may presuppose something untrue — an entity, office, award, event,
release, or occurrence that does not exist or has not happened (e.g. asking when someone won an award

Before writing the Final answer, FIRST re-examine the main question's PRESUPPOSITION against the
sub-answers. Make the presupposition explicit: a "what YEAR did X happen" question presupposes that X

Then ask: do the sub-answers ACTUALLY SUBSTANTIATE that presupposition? They substantiate it only if

fabrication and likewise do NOT substantiate the premise — do not treat a value invented to resolve a
contradiction as a confirmed fact. If the sub-answers fail to confirm the presupposition — they have no
record of it, are vague, contradictory, or mutually inconsistent on the core fact, or only establish a
related-but-different fact — then the premise is FALSE.
